 # E-Commerce Sales Performance Analysis

Project Overview: A comprehensive data analysis focused on unlocking sales trends and optimizing product distribution across key regional markets.

The Problem Statement:
Despite having a diverse product catalog, the business lacked a centralized view of its performance metrics. Key stakeholders were unable to identify:

-Which geographic regions were driving the most value?
-The discrepancy between high-volume low-cost items and high-value revenue drivers.
-Monthly seasonality patterns affecting inventory and logistics.
-Without these insights, the company faced risks of inefficient marketing spend and stock imbalances.

Methodology
To solve this, I implemented an end-to-end Business Intelligence workflow using Power BI:
Data Transformation: Cleaned and structured the raw sales data (addressing missing values and formatting date/time fields).
Data Modeling: Established relationships between product categories, city locations, and order dates.
DAX Calculations: Created measures for Total Revenue, Order Volume, and Average Item Price to drive the KPI ribbon.
Visualization: Developed an interactive dashboard focusing on spatial analysis (City maps), trend analysis (Line charts), and product rankings.


Measurable Key Insights
The analysis revealed a powerful story of Volume vs. Value:

- Financial Health: The business generated a total revenue of 34.49M from a catalog of 19 unique products.
The Hub of Revenue: San Francisco emerged as the primary growth engine, outperforming 8 other cities to become the highest revenue-generating location.
- The Revenue King: The MacBook Pro Laptop stands as the top financial performer, contributing 8.0M to total sales.
- The "Volume Trap" Paradox: * AAA Batteries (4 pack) recorded the highest quantity ordered among the top 5 products.

However, despite moving the most units, it fell into the bottom 5 for revenue generation due to its low price point.

Strategic Recommendation: High-volume items like batteries should be used as add-on suggestions at checkout to increase average order value, while marketing efforts should remain focused on high-margin items like the MacBook Pro.

Final Business Decision Impact
By identifying that 209K orders were spread across only 9 cities, the business can now:

- Targeted Marketing: Shift ad-spend toward San Francisco to maximize ROI.
- Inventory Optimization: Ensure high-volume items (Batteries) are stocked in decentralized warehouses near high-order cities to reduce shipping costs.
-Pricing Strategy: Evaluate if bundling low-revenue/high-volume items with premium electronics could further boost the 34.49M bottom line.
